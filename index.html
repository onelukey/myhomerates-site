The fundamentals

How a mortgage actually works

Most homeowners make their biggest financial decision without understanding the mechanics. Here's the short version.

1

You borrow the purchase price (minus your down payment)

The lender pays the seller. You now owe the lender that amount — plus interest — repaid over the loan term, typically 15 or 30 years.

2

Each monthly payment covers interest first, then principal

In the early years, most of your payment goes to interest. Over time, more goes to reducing the balance. This is called amortisation.

3

Your home is the collateral

If you stop paying, the lender can foreclose and sell the property to recover what they're owed. This is why lenders care so much about your ability to repay.

4

After 30 years of payments — you own it outright

The final payment clears the balance. No more mortgage. The equity you've built belongs to you.
